QUIETKAT 750

One of the smallest e-bikes currently manufactured by QuietKat, the QuietKat 750 Bandit Folding Frame Electric Bike is an ideal combination of portability and power. With both a folding steering column and a folding frame mechanism, the QuietKat Bandit can be reduced to just a fraction of its original size. Unlike many folding bikes, the QuietKat Bandit folding frame electric bike retains the strength and durability that has become synonymous with the QuietKat range of off-road electric bikes.

The QuietKat Ambush 750 LT is built on a solid, one-piece Aluminum frame. It includes an 8-gear Shimano shifter, mechanical brakes, and has a top speed of 19 mph – although I hit low 20’s during my review. The bike sports two 26” fat tires that absorb the shock and abuse found on off-road trails. I tested this bike in mud, sand, and gravel, and it traversed them easily. The bike contains a small gear motor that sits between the pedals. That motor is powered by a 48v Lithium-Ion battery. The power supplied to that motor is adjustable. Using a convenient power switch on the handlebar, you can set the bike power from 1 to 5, with 5 being the maximum power applied.

Easy to Fold

The QuietKat Bandit can be folded down for easy transport in the trunk of a car; then all you need to do is quickly and simply fold the frame and steering column back up to full size, and you’re ready to start riding.

Robust Design

Despite the folding frame design, the QuietKat Bandit e-bike is still tough enough to successfully carry a load of up to 300 lbs. This makes the QuietKat Bandit a great choice for those who want to haul cargo, game or tools in and out of inaccessible areas, all while crossing inhospitable terrain.

Optimum Power

One of the fantastic things about the QuietKat Bandit is that riders don’t have to compromise on power just because it is a folding bike. On the contrary, the QuietKat Bandit electric bike has an impressive 750-watt Bafang mid-drive motor with a 3-speed internal gearing system for maximum efficiency. Connected to a 48 volt, 11.6 amp hour Panasonic lithium battery, this electric motor has the ability to output up to 1.01 mechanical horsepower, a phenomenal effort when compared to the output of most electric bike motors. The motor can be engaged with a Class II throttle for instant power or by selecting one of the levels of PAS (Pedal Assist).

Superb Battery Life

The marketing specification claim that the Ambush 750 LT can go 20 miles on a full charge (10 bars). On day two of my review, I started with a fully charged battery and spent all day testing and filming. By the time I wrapped up, I had gone 18 miles with 3 bars remaining (30%) on the bike console. So, I believe that the marketing claims are accurate. Full discloser; battery life is tough to predict because conditions can vary widely. I don’t have many steep trails, and the temperatures were warm when I performed my tests. I would assume that if you were taking the bike up Pike’s Peak in Colorado, the battery discharge rate may be different. A fully drained battery takes 4-6 hours to charge. The charging can take place on the bike, or you can remove the battery and charge it indoors. The QuietKat bike includes a charger designed for the 48v battery.
